Why optimize geometries?

Any property you calculate from theoritical chemistry will require a structure. From a mathematical view, the energy of a molecule is a function of the coordinates of its atoms. The probability of a molecule having a particular geometry can be described by a Boltzmann population. Therefore, the structures you are most likely to see (and measure in a labrotory setting) are located at an energy minima. So, if we want to simulate something comparable to the real world, we want to use a structure close to the energy minimum.
